Fun fact: Most solar systems produce more electricity than a home needs. If you live in a state with net metering policies, the utility company buys your extra energy at the full retail rate of electricity.

Your payment would come in the form of credits. In the wintertime when there isn’t as much sun, credits could keep your electric bill low.1 		 
 
 
How Net Metering Works
 
 
Solar Panels
 
Transform energy
from the sun into
electric current.
 
Inverter
 
Converts electric
current into electricity
your home can use.
 
Your Home
 
Uses the energy it
needs and sends the
excess to the grid.
 
 
Electric Meter
 
Monitors the electricity
you use and how much
your panels produce.
 
Electric Grid
 
Your excess energy goes to the
grid. When you need more
energy than your panels
produce, energy comes to you.
 
 
  Net metering is what makes rooftop solar panels such a great way to save money! In fact, the best states for installing solar aren’t necessarily the ones that get the most sunshine – they’re the ones with the best net metering policies.
